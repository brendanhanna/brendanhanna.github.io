Clear definitions, a mental model for how this works, and why ‚Äúyou + AI‚Äù is the point ‚Äî not replacing you.

Say this: ‚ÄúWhen people say ‚ÄòAI‚Äô right now, they almost always mean large language models ‚Äî LLMs ‚Äî like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity. Let‚Äôs put some clean definitions on the board and show how they actually work.‚Äù

1. What do we mean by ‚ÄúAI‚Äù today?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a broad umbrella: anything where computers perform tasks that typically require human intelligence (perception, reasoning, language, planning).

Large Language Models (LLMs) are a specific kind of AI trained on huge amounts of text and code to predict the ‚Äúnext token‚Äù (piece of text).

In practice, when people say ‚ÄúAI‚Äù at work today, they usually mean ‚Äúan LLM-powered assistant‚Äù (ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, Claude, etc.).

üß©

2. How does an LLM actually work?

Very compressed mental model:

  • Training: read a huge corpus of text + code
  • Goal: become extremely good at predicting the next token
  • Result: the model learns patterns of language, reasoning, and world knowledge
  • At run-time: you give it a prompt ‚Üí it predicts the next token repeatedly ‚Üí you see fluent text

LLMs are basically powerful pattern-compressors over text. The Karpathy video linked above goes deep on this if you want more.

ü§ù

3. Human-in-the-loop

LLMs are pattern engines, not oracles. They are amazing at:

  • Drafting and redrafting text
  • Explaining complex topics in plain language
  • Summarizing long material
  • Generating options you can choose from

Your job: provide context, judgment, and guardrails. Treat AI outputs as ‚ÄúVersion 0‚Äù ‚Äî you decide what ships.

üî§

Basic vocabulary

Words we‚Äôll use later without re-defining
  • Prompt: what you type / say to the model
  • RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): LLM + your documents (e.g., policies, protocols, SOPs)
  • Context window: how much text the model can ‚Äúsee‚Äù at once
  • Agent: an LLM that can call tools (search, email, databases) and perform multi-step workflows
Example:
Explain the difference between AI and an LLM to a new hire in 3 bullet points.
üåÄ

Why LLMs sometimes ‚Äúhallucinate‚Äù

LLMs are very good next-token predictors, not verified knowledge bases. They:

  • Don‚Äôt have built-in citations by default
  • Will fill in gaps with plausible-sounding text
  • Can be over-confident in wrong answers

We mitigate this with RAG, tools like Perplexity, NotebookLM, Copilot notebooks, and your review.

Example:
Here is a policy / SOP. Summarize key steps and flag anything ambiguous or risky.

The AI Adoption Gradient

A simple way to think about AI opportunities ‚Äî from ‚ÄúI just talk to a chatbot‚Äù to ‚Äúwe build custom, integrated solutions.‚Äù Works for both individual and corporate adoption.

Say this: ‚ÄúYou don‚Äôt have to jump to ‚Äòfully custom platform‚Äô on day one. Most of the value is in Levels 1 and 2. Levels 3 and 4 are where we invest in more structured workflows and custom development.‚Äù
Level 1
Personal Productivity
Talk to an AI assistant. Draft, summarize, explain.
Level 2
Team Knowledge Assistants
AI that knows your docs and context.
Level 3
Automated Workflows & Agents
Multi-step tasks with tools and integrations.
Level 4
Custom Enterprise AI
Bespoke solutions, integrated into core systems.

Level 1 ‚Äì Personal Productivity

Tools: ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, Claude (no special setup).

  • Drafting emails, documents, slide outlines
  • Summarizing long emails / threads / meeting notes
  • Explaining complex scientific or business topics
  • Brainstorming options and pros/cons
You are my personal assistant at Astria. Given this email / document, propose a response in my voice, with 3 options of tone: direct, neutral, and warm.

Level 2 ‚Äì Team Knowledge Assistants

Tools: NotebookLM, Copilot notebooks, internal RAG pilots.

  • Q&A over SOPs, policies, and training material
  • Onboarding ‚Äúcopilot‚Äù for new hires
  • Search across scattered docs with plain language
  • Create FAQs, cheat sheets, and checklists
Here are 10 SOPs related to device manufacturing. Build a step-by-step checklist and flag any conflicting instructions.

Level 3 ‚Äì Automated Workflows & Agents

Tools: Copilot Studio, Power Automate, custom agents.

  • Intake & triage (IT, HR, facilities requests)
  • Simple ‚Äúconcierge‚Äù bots that call your systems
  • Automated report generation on a schedule
  • Routing approvals and nudging people to act
Design an agent for IT ticket triage that reads the ticket, tags it, suggests a solution, and routes to the right queue.

Level 4 ‚Äì Custom Enterprise AI

Tools: Azure OpenAI, custom backends, full-stack apps.

  • Fully integrated pharmacovigilance or device-monitoring tools
  • Custom decision-support dashboards
  • Complex, cross-system workflow orchestration
  • Advanced governance, auditing, and compliance controls
Propose a phased plan to move from ‚Äúad-hoc AI experiments‚Äù to a governed, Azure-hosted enterprise AI platform at Astria.
